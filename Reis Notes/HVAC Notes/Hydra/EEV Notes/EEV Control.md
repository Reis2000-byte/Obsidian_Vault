- Super Heat Calculation:
	- Measure Suction pressure
	- Convert pressure to saturation temp using a PT chart for the refrigerant
	- Measure suction line temp
	- Superheat = Line Temp - Saturation Temp

- Low Superheat (0–5°F) gives risk of liquid refrigerant returning to the compressor
- Normal (~10–15°F typical, depends on system) is good
- High Superheat (20°F+) evaporator may be underfed (low charge, restriction, airflow issue)
	- This can mean that the refrigerant finished boiling too early in the evaporator and the remaining coil is just heating water vapor

- Desired Superheat range is 2-20°F with a target of 10°F in normal operation

- Low Superheat is bad because you are at risk of damaging the compressor.
- High Superheat is bad because your capacity tanks. Most of the heat transfer is at the phase change which you want occurring while the refrigerant is in the evaporator coil.

- Open EEV:
	- High Superheat
	- Start Up
- Close EEV:
	- Low Superheat
	- System Idle
