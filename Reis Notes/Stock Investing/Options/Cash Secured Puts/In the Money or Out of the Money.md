### 🎯 The Short Answer

**For selling CSPs — you want to be OUT of the money (OTM).**

But let me explain what that actually means because "in the money" and "out of the money" confuse almost every beginner at first.

---

### 📍 What Does In/Out of the Money Mean?

Let's say SOFI is trading at **$15.**

|Strike Price|Status|What It Means|
|---|---|---|
|**$18 put**|In the Money (ITM)|Strike is ABOVE current price|
|**$15 put**|At the Money (ATM)|Strike equals current price|
|**$13 put**|Out of the Money (OTM)|Strike is BELOW current price|
|**$10 put**|Deep OTM|Strike is far below current price|

Think of it like a limbo bar — the current stock price is the bar. OTM means your strike is safely **below** the bar.

---

### 🟢 Why OTM is Better for CSP Sellers

When you sell a CSP, you're hoping the stock **never reaches your strike.** The further OTM you are, the more cushion you have before things go wrong.

Here's the tradeoff though:

|Position|Premium|Safety|Risk|
|---|---|---|---|
|**Deep ITM**|Very high|Very low|Likely assigned|
|**ATM**|High|Low|Coin flip|
|**Slightly OTM**|Moderate|Good|Manageable|
|**Deep OTM**|Very low|Very high|Almost never assigned|

---

### ⚖️ The Sweet Spot

For a moderate risk trader like you, the sweet spot is **slightly OTM** — specifically targeting a **delta of 0.20 to 0.30.**

#### What is Delta here?

Delta on a put tells you the approximate probability of being assigned. So:

- **Delta 0.20** = roughly 20% chance of assignment = 80% chance of profit
- **Delta 0.30** = roughly 30% chance of assignment = 70% chance of profit

This range gives you **decent premium without crazy risk.** It's the most common zone professional sellers operate in.

### 🚨 The One Time ITM Makes Sense

The only scenario experienced traders sell ITM puts is when they **actually want to own the stock** and are trying to get assigned at a specific price while collecting premium along the way. But for income generation — which is your goal right now — **stay OTM.**

---

### 🔑 The One Rule to Remember

> **Sell OTM puts on stocks you wouldn't mind owning, at a price you'd be happy to buy them at.**

That one sentence captures the entire CSP philosophy. You're getting paid to wait for a stock to come to your price — and if it never does, you just keep the premium and do it again.
