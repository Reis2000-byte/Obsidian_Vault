
### 🏆 The Liquidity Checklist

Before entering ANY CSP run through this quick checklist:

```
✅ Bid/Ask spread under $0.15?
✅ Open Interest above 500?
✅ Daily options volume above 100?
✅ Stock trades over 1M shares per day?
✅ Can I get filled near the midpoint?
```

If you can check all five boxes — you have a liquid, tradeable options market.

### 🔍 The 5 Ways to Judge Liquidity

#### 1️⃣ Bid/Ask Spread — Most Important

This is your **#1 liquidity indicator.** Remember the screenshot you showed me earlier with Bid $0.00 and Ask $0.75? That's a nightmare spread.

|Spread Size|Liquidity|Verdict|
|---|---|---|
|**$0.01 – $0.05**|Excellent|✅ Trade it|
|**$0.05 – $0.15**|Good|✅ Fine to trade|
|**$0.15 – $0.30**|Okay|⚠️ Proceed carefully|
|**$0.30 – $0.75**|Poor|❌ Avoid|
|**$0.75+**|Terrible|🚨 Walk away|

**Why does a wide spread hurt you?**

Say the bid is $0.10 and ask is $0.85. The midpoint is $0.47. You sell at the mid and collect $47. But when you go to buy it back at 50% profit, the spread has shifted and you're forced to pay $0.40 instead of $0.23. The wide spread just ate your profit.

---

#### 2️⃣ Open Interest (OI)

Open Interest is the **total number of contracts currently open** on that specific strike.

|Open Interest|Liquidity|
|---|---|
|**Above 500**|✅ Good|
|**Above 1,000**|✅ Very good|
|**Above 5,000**|✅ Excellent|
|**Below 100**|❌ Poor — avoid|
|**Single digits**|🚨 Walk away|

You can see this directly on Robinhood's options chain. It's listed right next to each strike price.

**Why it matters:** High open interest means lots of traders are active at that strike — which means there's almost always someone willing to take the other side of your trade quickly.

---

#### 3️⃣ Volume

Volume is **how many contracts have traded TODAY specifically.**

|Daily Volume|Liquidity|
|---|---|
|**500+ contracts**|✅ Active market|
|**100–500**|⚠️ Moderate|
|**Under 50**|❌ Thin market|
|**0–10**|🚨 Almost no activity|

**OI vs Volume — what's the difference?**

- OI = all open contracts ever (like total players in a game)
- Volume = contracts traded just today (like how many are actively playing right now)

You want both to be healthy — not just one.

---

#### 4️⃣ The Underlying Stock's Daily Volume

Before you even look at the options chain, check **how many shares of the stock itself trade per day.**

|Stock Daily Volume|Options Liquidity|
|---|---|
|**10M+ shares/day**|✅ Almost always liquid options|
|**1M – 10M**|✅ Usually fine|
|**500K – 1M**|⚠️ Check options carefully|
|**Under 500K**|❌ Likely poor options liquidity|

This is why QQQ, SOFI, RIVN, and SOUN have tradeable options — they all have millions of shares trading daily. Tiny obscure stocks with low volume will almost always have terrible options liquidity.

---

#### 5️⃣ The "Fill Test" — Real World Check

When you're about to place a trade on Robinhood:

1. Pull up the options chain
2. Look at the bid/ask spread
3. Try placing a **limit order at the midpoint**
4. If it fills within 30 seconds → good liquidity
5. If it sits there for minutes with no fill → poor liquidity

You can always cancel before it fills. This is the real world gut check that experienced traders use.
